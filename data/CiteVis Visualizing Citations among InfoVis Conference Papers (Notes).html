CiteVis Paper and Citation Notes

In counting and logging the citations between the years of IEEE InfoVis papers, we encountered some issues and problems within Google Scholar. Below we identify the "issues" that we found. These fall into two categories. First, the proper IEEE InfoVis conference paper simply does not appear (on its own) in Google Scholar. Typically in this case, the paper has been merged with another similar paper. In each of these cases, we note below the paper that we included in CiteVis. Second, for a number of the InfoVis papers, an alternate, often longer version of the paper was published at a different venue. For example, from 2001 to 2005, the authors of some of the best papers at the conference were asked to prepare a longer version for either IEEE Trans. on Visualization and Computer Graphics or Information Visualization. In many cases, these longer versions of a paper have received more citations over time. For these papers, CiteVis still only includes citations to the actual IEEE InfoVis conference paper.
